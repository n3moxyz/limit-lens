## Design Context

### Users
Limit Lens is for AI power users who use Codex and Claude throughout the day and need a calm way to monitor rate limits, decide when to switch tools or models, and avoid wasting scarce Codex capacity. It also needs to support short, reliable hack-night demos, but the daily-driver workflow is the priority.

### Brand Personality
Quiet, utilitarian, and trustworthy. The interface should feel like a precise desktop instrument: calm under pressure, readable at a glance, and never performative.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use native macOS conventions, restrained visual hierarchy, compact density, and system-adaptive materials. The app should feel polished and practical rather than flashy. Demo affordances should be present but subtle, secondary, and easy to ignore during daily use.

### Design Principles
- Lead with the user's next action, not raw telemetry.
- Keep monitoring glanceable, compact, and low-noise.
- Separate daily-driver controls from demo-only controls.
- Make failure and permission states actionable without clutter.
- Preserve native macOS behavior, accessibility, and keyboard-friendly interaction.
